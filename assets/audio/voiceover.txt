Millimeter-wave radar can estimate human pose without cameras. But when it encounters just one unseen motion, like a left-hand wave, joint error can jump from about fifty to over one hundred and twenty millimeters.

Collect more data? Not so fast. In today's largest datasets, removing seventy percent of the samples costs less than two percent accuracy. The real bottleneck is coverage, not volume.

WiCompass turns large motion-capture corpora into an oracle for radar data collection. It learns a compact vocabulary of human motion, and maps the pose labels of existing radar datasets into this space.

This reveals which motions are missing, and which samples are redundant.

WiCompass then selects the most informative target poses. A participant mimics them for real radar capture, or a simulator generates the corresponding returns. The new samples close the coverage gaps, and the loop repeats.

Across data budgets from two to forty thousand simulated samples, WiCompass reduces out-of-distribution error by twenty-five to thirty millimeters. Under the same eight-thousand-frame real-world budget, test error falls from one hundred twelve point nine, to one hundred five point seven millimeters.
