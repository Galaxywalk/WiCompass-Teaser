From Peking University, UESTC, Pitt, and MIT: Wi-Compass, Oracle-driven Data Scaling for millimeter-wave Human Pose Estimation. We use data coverage to choose which motions to collect.

Across actions, current models fail to generalize. Holding out left-hand wave raises error from 41.1 to 151.1 millimeters.

On mmBody, thirty percent of the data still yields sixty-eight millimeters. More data alone does not scale performance.

An A-MASS-trained pose tokenizer maps motion-capture poses and radar pose labels into one shared space, without aligning raw signals.

Directional coverage finds A-MASS poses missing from radar data. Capped sampling favors sparse gaps without letting outliers dominate.

From two thousand to forty thousand samples, the fitted scaling laws remain twenty-five to thirty millimeters apart.

Training errors match. On held-out poses, Wi-Compass reaches 105.7 millimeters, versus 112.9 from an action list.

For robust millimeter-wave pose estimation, use motion-capture priors to find missing motions and guide radar collection.
